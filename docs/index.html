Help and Instructions

Welcome to Unveiling Patterns: A Data-Driven Insight into Global Terrorism. This website is designed to provide an interactive, comprehensive view of global terrorism patterns from 1970 to 2020. Here's how you can interact with the data and visuals:

  • Group Profiles: Click on this button to explore the data related to the ten deadliest terrorist organizations. You'll see two visuals: a bar chart and a heatmap. The bar chart depicts the number of fatalities associated with each group. Hover over the bars for a detailed view of the numbers. The heatmap presents the commonly used attack methods for these groups, color intensity indicating frequency.
  • Attack Hotspots: This feature lets you explore the global distribution of terrorist attacks for a selected year. Use the slider to change the year and the map will update accordingly. Each circle on the map represents an attack, color-coded by the target type. Click on a circle to view more information about the attack, including the target, country, terrorist group involved, and a brief incident summary (if available).
  • Temporal Patterns: Here you can find a heatmap showing the distribution of attacks throughout the calendar year. The x-axis represents months, and the y-axis represents days of the month. Each cell in the heatmap represents the total number of attacks that occurred on that specific day across all years in the dataset. The color intensity indicates the number of attacks, with brighter colors representing higher attack counts.
  • Regional Impact: This feature lets you analyze the terrorism trends in a specific region. Choose a region from the dropdown menu. The map will highlight your chosen region. Below the map, you'll find a scatterplot showcasing all fatal attacks within that region, with the x-axis representing the years and the y-axis indicating the number of casualties. Hover over a point for detailed information about the incident.
  • Country-Specific Analysis: To view terrorism patterns for a specific country, select a country from the dropdown menu. The map will highlight your chosen country and a streamgraph will appear below. The streamgraph displays the frequency of attacks throughout the years, with the x-axis representing months and the stream's width indicating the number of attacks.

If you need further assistance or have any questions, please consult our guidebook or contact us.

Welcome to 'Unveiling Patterns: A Data-Driven Insight into Global Terrorism'

This platform provides an interactive exploration of data from the Global Terrorism Database (GTD), covering the span of half a century from 1970 to 2020. Please note that data for the year 1973 is missing from the GTD.

Through this visualization portal, we aim to present an unbiased, comprehensive view of global terrorism patterns, focusing on diverse aspects such as attack frequency, casualties, seasonality, regional trends, methods, and targets of attacks. The data can be explored via various interactive visualizations, each carefully designed to convey specific aspects of the data. This includes an overview of terrorist groups and their preferred methods of attack, a geographically focused view of attack density, trend analysis, region-specific exploration, and a country-wise deep-dive.

By presenting this data, we hope to foster an understanding of the patterns and impacts of terrorism on a global scale. The information should be handled with care due to the sensitive nature of the subject matter. We encourage you to navigate through the visualizations and uncover the hidden patterns of global terrorism.

Understanding patterns in terrorism is crucial for various stakeholders - from policymakers and security agencies to researchers and citizens. It allows us to comprehend the scope of this global issue, identify hotspots, discern trends, and possibly, infer underlying causes and implications. The power of data visualization is employed here to present this complex and expansive data in an intuitive and interactive manner, facilitating better comprehension and deeper analysis.

The objective of this project is to provide an accessible tool for understanding the landscape of global terrorism, thereby contributing to informed discussions, policy making, and educational endeavors. We uphold the principle of data neutrality, providing an unbiased view and leaving room for interpretation based on the data.

Feel free to start your journey by selecting any of the five categories. Should you require assistance in navigating through the website, please click on the 'Help' button on the top right corner for guidance.
